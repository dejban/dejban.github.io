"Surely you're joking, Mr. Feynman!"

July 5, 2017

Richard Feynman tells the story of his adventurous life from childhood to the years after his Nobel prize. Apart from his genius, you will learn a lot about his childish honesty, persistence in learning (whether it is physics, music, drawing, or a new language), intuitive problem solving, humility, and of course sense of humor. The most important thing I learned from his autobiography is his first principle thinking or skepticism towards the "experts" as he mentions: "... I never pay any attention to anything by experts. I calculate everything myself."
